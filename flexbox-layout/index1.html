Me Before You

Jojo Moyes' novel "Me Before You," adapted into a film, tells the story of an emotional bond between two individuals.
Louisa's World and New Beginnings: Louisa Clark, a young woman with a unique style, finds a job as a caregiver for Will Traynor.
Will's Decision: Will is paralyzed and plans assisted suicide. Louisa tries to change his mind.
Bittersweet Love: They fall in love, but Will remains committed to his decision.
Aftermath: Will’s final letter inspires Louisa to live a full life.

Pride and Prejudice

"Pride and Prejudice" follows the relationship between Elizabeth Bennet and Mr. Darcy, exploring themes like societal expectations, personal growth, and love.
  • Elizabeth rejects societal norms and seeks love based on mutual respect.
  • Mr. Darcy overcomes his pride, Elizabeth her prejudice.
  • The novel highlights the challenges faced by women in 19th century England.
Jane Austen’s timeless novel shows how first impressions can be misleading — and how love can flourish beyond social conventions.
